dual module


Let R be a ring and M be a left http://planetmath.org/node/365R-module. The dual module of M is the right http://planetmath.org/node/365R-module consisting of all module homomorphisms from M into R.

It is denoted by M∗. The elements of M∗ are called linear functionals.

The action of R on M∗ is given by (f⁢r)⁢(m)=(f⁢(m))⁢r for f∈M∗, m∈M, and r∈R.

If R is commutative, then every M is an http://planetmath.org/node/987(R,R)-bimodule with r⁢m=m⁢r for all r∈R and m∈M. Hence, it makes sense to ask whether M and M∗ are isomorphic. Suppose that b:M×M→R is a bilinear form. Then it is easy to check that for a fixed m∈M, the function b⁢(m,-):M→R is a module homomorphism, so is an element of M∗. Then we have a module homomorphism from M to M∗ given by m↦b⁢(m,-).
