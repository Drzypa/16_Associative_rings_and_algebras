nested ideals in von Neumann regular ring


Theorem.

Let 𝔞 be an ideal of the von Neumann regular ring R.  Then 𝔞 itself is a von Neumann regular ring and any ideal 𝔟 of 𝔞 is likewise an ideal of R.

Proof.

If  a∈𝔞, then  a⁢s⁢a=a  for some  s∈R.  Setting  t=s⁢a⁢s  we see that t belongs to the ideal 𝔞 and

a⁢t⁢a=a⁢(s⁢a⁢s)⁢a=(a⁢s⁢a)⁢s⁢a=a⁢s⁢a=a.

Secondly, we have to show that whenever  b∈𝔟⊆𝔞  and  r∈R, then both b⁢r and r⁢b lie in 𝔟.  Now,  b⁢r∈𝔞  because 𝔞 is an ideal of R.  Thus there is an element x in 𝔞 satisfying  b⁢r⁢x⁢b⁢r=b⁢r.  Since r⁢x⁢b⁢r belongs to 𝔞 and 𝔟 is assumed to be an ideal of 𝔞, we conclude that the product  b⋅r⁢x⁢b⁢r  must lie in 𝔟, i.e.  b⁢r∈𝔟.  Similarly it can be shown that  r⁢b∈𝔟. ∎
