orthogonal idempotents of the group ring


Let G be a finite abelian group, let L be any field containing the |G|-th roots of unity, and let G^ denote the character group of G with values in L. For any character χ∈G^, we define εχ, the corresponding orthogonal idempotent of the group ring L⁢[G], by

εχ=1|G|⁢∑g∈Gχ⁢(g)⁢g-1.

The following equalities hold:

  • •

    εχ2=εχ for all χ

  • •

    εχ⁢εψ=0 for any χ≠ψ

  • •

    ∑χ∈G^εχ=1

  • •

    εχ⋅g=χ⁢(g)⁢εχ

These orthogonal idempotents are used to decompose modules over L⁢[G]: If M is such a module, then M=⊕χ(εχ⁢M).
