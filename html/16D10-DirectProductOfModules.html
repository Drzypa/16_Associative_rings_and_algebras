direct product of modules


Let {Xi:i∈I} be a collection of modules in some category of modules. Then the direct product ∏i∈IXi of that collection is the module whose underlying set is the Cartesian product of the Xi with componentwise addition and scalar multiplication. For example, in a category of left modules:

(xi)+(yi)=(xi+yi),
r⁢(xi)=(r⁢xi).

For each j∈I we have a projection pj:∏i∈IXi→Xj defined by (xi)↦xj, and an injection λj:Xj→∏i∈IXi where an element xj of Xj maps to the element of ∏i∈IXi whose jth term is xj and every other term is zero.

The direct product ∏i∈IXi satisfies a certain universal property. Namely, if Y is a module and there exist homomorphisms fi:Xi→Y for all i∈I, then there exists a unique homomorphism ϕ:Y→∏i∈IXi satisfying ϕ⁢λi=fi for all i∈I.
