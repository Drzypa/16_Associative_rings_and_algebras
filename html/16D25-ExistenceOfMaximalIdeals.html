existence of maximal ideals


Theorem.

Let R be a unital ring. Every proper ideal of R lies in a maximal ideal of R.

Applying this theorem to the zero ideal gives the following corollary:

Corollary.

Every unital ring R≠0 has a maximal ideal.

Proof of theorem. This proof is a straightforward application of Zorn’s Lemma. Readers are encouraged to attempt the proof themselves before reading the details below.

Let ℐ be a proper ideal of ℛ, and let Σ be the partially ordered set

Σ={𝒜∣𝒜⁢ is an ideal of ⁢ℛ, and ⁢ℐ⊆𝒜≠ℛ}

ordered by inclusion.

Note that ℐ∈Σ, so Σ is non-empty.

In order to apply Zorn’s Lemma we need to prove that every non-empty chain (http://planetmath.org/TotalOrder) in Σ has an upper bound in Σ. Let {𝒜α} be a non-empty chain of ideals in Σ, so for all indices α,β we have

𝒜α⊆𝒜β  or  𝒜β⊆𝒜α.

We claim that ℬ defined by

ℬ=⋃α𝒜α

is a suitable upper bound.

  • •

    ℬ is an ideal. Indeed, let a,b∈ℬ, so there exist α,β such that a∈𝒜α, b∈𝒜β. Since these two ideals are in a totally ordered chain we have

    𝒜α⊆𝒜β  or  𝒜β⊆𝒜α

    Without loss of generality, we assume 𝒜α⊆𝒜β. Then both a,b∈𝒜β, and 𝒜β is an ideal of the ring ℛ. Thus a+b∈𝒜β⊆ℬ.

    Similarly, let r∈ℛ and b∈ℬ. As above, there exists β such that b∈𝒜β. Since 𝒜β is an ideal we have

    r⋅b∈𝒜β⊆ℬ

    and

    b⋅r∈𝒜β⊆ℬ.

    Therefore, ℬ is an ideal.

  • •

    ℬ≠ℛ, otherwise 1 would belong to ℬ, so there would be an α such that 1∈𝒜α so 𝒜α=ℛ. But this is impossible because we assumed 𝒜α∈Σ for all indices α.

  • •

    ℐ⊆ℬ. Indeed, the chain is non-empty, so there is some 𝒜α in the chain, and we have ℐ⊆𝒜α⊆ℬ.

Therefore ℬ∈Σ. Hence every chain in Σ has an upper bound in Σ and we can apply Zorn’s Lemma to deduce the existence of ℳ, a maximal element (with respect to inclusion) in Σ. By definition of the set Σ, this ℳ must be a maximal ideal of ℛ containing ℐ. QED

Note that the above proof never makes use of the associativity of ring multiplication, and the result therefore holds also in non-associative rings. The result cannot, however, be generalized to rings without unity.

Note also that the use of the Axiom of Choice (in the form of Zorn’s Lemma) is necessary, as there are models of ZF in which the above theorem and corollary fail.
