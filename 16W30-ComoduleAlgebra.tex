Let $H$ be a bialgebra.
A \textbf{right $H$-comodule algebra} is a unital algebra $A$ which is a right $H$-comodule
satisfying
\begin{equation}
t(ab) = t(a)t(b) = \sum a_{(1)}b_{(1)} \otimes a_{(2)}b_{(2)}, \quad
t(\identity_A) = \identity_A\otimes\identity_H,
\end{equation}
for all $h \in H$ and $a,b \in A$.

There is a dual notion of a $H$-module coalgebra.

\begin{example}
Let $H$ be a bialgebra.
Then $H$ is itself a $H$-comodule algebra for the right regular coaction
$t(h) = \Delta(h)$.
\end{example}
