subcoalgebras and coideals


Let (C,Δ,ε) be a coalgebra over a field k.

Definition. Vector subspace D⊆C is called subcoalgebra iff Δ⁢(D)⊆D⊗D.

Definition. Vector subspace I⊆C is is called coideal iff Δ⁢(I)⊆I⊗C+C⊗I and ε⁢(I)=0.

One can show that if D⊆C is a subcoalgebra, then (D,Δ|D,ε|D) is also a coalgebra. On the other hand, if I⊆C is a coideal, then we can cannoicaly introduce a coalgebra structure on the quotient space C/I. More precisely, if x∈C and Δ⁢(x)=∑ai⊗bi, then we define

Δ′:C/I→(C/I)⊗(C/I);
Δ′⁢(x+I)=∑(ai+I)⊗(bi+I)

and ε′:C/I→k as ε′⁢(x+I)=ε⁢(x). One can show that these two maps are well defined and (C/I,Δ′,ε′) is a coalgebra.
