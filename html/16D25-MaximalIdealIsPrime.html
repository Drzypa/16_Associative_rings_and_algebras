maximal ideal is prime


Theorem. In a commutative ring with non-zero unity, any maximal ideal is a prime ideal.

Proof.  Let 𝔪 be a maximal ideal of such a ring R and let the ring product r⁢s belong to 𝔪 but e.g.  r∉𝔪. The maximality of 𝔪 implies that  𝔪+(r)=R=(1).  Thus there exists an element  m∈𝔪  and an element  x∈R  such that  m+x⁢r=1.  Now m and r⁢s belong to 𝔪, whence

s=1⁢s=(m+x⁢r)⁢s=s⁢m+x⁢(r⁢s)∈𝔪.

So we can say that along with r⁢s, at least one of its factors (http://planetmath.org/Product) belongs to 𝔪, and therefore 𝔪 is a prime ideal of R.
