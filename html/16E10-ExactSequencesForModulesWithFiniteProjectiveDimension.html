exact sequences for modules with finite projective dimension


Proposition. Let R be a ring and M be a (left) R-module, such that proj dim⁢(M)=n<∞. If

0→K→Pn→⋯→P0→M→0

is an exact sequence of R-modules, such that each Pi is projective, then K is projective.

Proof. Since proj dim⁢(M)=n<∞, then there exists exact sequence of R-modules

0→Pn′→⋯→P0′→M→0,

Note that sequences

Pn→⋯→P0→M→0;
Pn′→⋯→P0→M→0,

are projective resolutions of M. Let δ:Pn→Pn-1 and β:Pn′→Pn-1′ be maps take from these resolutions. Then generalized Schanuel’s lemma implies that ker⁢δ and ker⁢β are projectively equivalent. But ker⁢δ≃K and ker⁢β=0. This means, that there are projective modules P,Q such that

K⊕P≃Q.

Therefore K is a direct summand of a free module (since Q is), which completes the proof. □
