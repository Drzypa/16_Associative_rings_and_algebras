superalgebra


A graded algebra A is said to be a superalgebra if it has a ℤ/2⁢ℤ grading. As a vector space, a superalgebra has a decomposition into two homogeneous subspaces, A=A0⊕A1. The homogeneous subspace A0 is known as the space of even elements of A, and A1 is known as the space of odd elements. Let |a| denote the degree of a homogeneous element. That is, |a|=0 if a∈A0 and |a|=1 if a∈A1. The degree satisfies |a⁢b|=|a|+|b|.
