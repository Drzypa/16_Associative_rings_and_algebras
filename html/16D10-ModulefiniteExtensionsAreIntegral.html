module-finite extensions are integral


Theorem Suppose B⊂A is module-finite. Then A is integral over B.

Proof. Choose u∈A.

For clarity, assume A is spanned by two elements ω1,ω2. The proof given clearly generalizes to the case where a spanning set for A has more than two elements.

Write

u⁢ω1 =b11⁢ω1+b12⁢ω2
u⁢ω2 =b21⁢ω1+b22⁢ω2

Consider

C=(u-b11-b12-b21u-b22)

and let Cadj be the adjugate of C. Then C⁢(ω1ω2)=0, so Cadj⁢C⁢(ω1ω2)=0.

Now, Cadj⁢C is a diagonal matrix with det⁡C on the diagonal, so

(f⁢(u)00f⁢(u))⁢(ω1ω2)=(00)

where f∈B⁢[x] is monic.

But neither ω1 nor ω2 is zero, so f⁢(u) must be.

Note that, as with the field case, the converse is not true. For example, the algebraic integers are integral but not finite over ℤ.
