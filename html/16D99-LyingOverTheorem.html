Lying-Over Theorem


Let 𝔬 be a subring of a commutative ring 𝔒 with nonzero unity and integral over 𝔬.  If 𝔞 is an ideal of 𝔬 and 𝔄 an ideal of 𝔒 such that

𝔄∩𝔬=𝔞,

then 𝔄 is said to lie over 𝔞.

Theorem.  If 𝔭 is a prime ideal of a ring 𝔬 which is a subring of a commutative ring 𝔒 with nonzero unity and integral over 𝔬, then there exists a prime ideal 𝔓 of 𝔒 lying over 𝔭.  If the prime ideals 𝔓 and 𝔔 both lie over 𝔭 and  𝔓⊆𝔔,  then  𝔓=𝔔.
