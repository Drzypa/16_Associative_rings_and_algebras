Let $F$ be a field and $K$ a finitely-generated commutative algebra over $F$.  There there exists a non-negative integer $n$ and elements $x_1,\ldots,x_n\in A$, algebraically independent over $F$, such that $K$ is integral over $F[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$.

% $K:=F[x_1,\ldots,x_i]$ for some $i\in\mathbb{N}$ and unrelated indeterminants $x_1,\ldots,x_i$. Let $t$ be the transcendence degree of $K$ over $F$. Then $\exists k_1,\ldots,k_t\in K:k_1,\ldots,k_t$ algebraically independent over $F$ and $K$ is \PMlinkid{integral}{1295} over $F[k_1,\ldots,k_t]$, i.e. for every $k\in K$, $\exists g\in F[x]$ such that $g$ is monic and $g(k)=0$.
