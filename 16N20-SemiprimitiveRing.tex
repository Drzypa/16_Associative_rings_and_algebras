A ring is said to be \emph{semiprimitive} if its Jacobson radical is the zero ideal.

Any simple ring is automatically semiprimitive.

A finite direct product of matrix rings over division rings can be shown to be semiprimitive and both left and right Artinian.

The \PMlinkname{Artin-Wedderburn Theorem}{WedderburnArtinTheorem} states that any semiprimitive ring which is left or right Artinian is isomorphic to a finite direct product of matrix rings over division rings.

{\bf Note}:
The semiprimitive condition is sometimes also referred to as a \emph{semisimple}, \emph{Jacobson semisimple}, or \emph{J-semisimple}.  Furthermore, when either of the last two names are used, the adjective 'semisimple' is frequently intended to refer to a ring that is semiprimitive and Artinian (see the entry on \PMlinkname{semisimple rings}{SemisimpleRing2}).
