center (rings)


If A is a ring, the center of A, sometimes denoted Z⁡(A), is the set of all elements in A that commute with all other elements of A. That is,

Z⁡(A)={a∈A∣a⁢x=x⁢a⁢⁢∀x∈A}

Note that 0∈Z⁡(A) so the center is non-empty. If we assume that A is a ring with a multiplicative unity 1, then 1 is in the center as well. The center of A is also a subring of A.
