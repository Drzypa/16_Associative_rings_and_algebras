direct sum


Let {Xi:i∈I} be a collection of modules in some category of modules. Then the direct sum ∐i∈IXi of that collection is the submodule of the direct product (http://planetmath.org/DirectProduct) of the Xi consisting of all elements (xi) such that all but a finite number of the xi are zero.

For each j∈I we have a projection pj:∐i∈IXi→Xj defined by (xi)↦xj, and an injection λj:Xj→∐i∈IXi where an element xj of Xj maps to the element of ∐i∈IXi whose jth term is xj and every other term is zero.

The direct sum ∐i∈IXi satisfies a certain universal property. Namely, if Y is a module and there exist homomorphisms fi:Y→Xi for all i∈I, then there exists a unique homomorphism ϕ:∐i∈IXi→Y satisfying pi⁢ϕ=fi for all i∈I.

\xymatrixXi&&Y\ar[ll]fi&∐i∈IXi\ar[ul]pi\ar@-->[ur]ϕ

The direct sum is often referred to as the weak direct sum or simply the sum.

Compare this to the direct product of modules.

Often an internal direct sum is written as ⊕i∈IXi.
